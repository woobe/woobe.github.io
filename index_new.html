About Me

Key moments in data science journey.

  • 2001-2004

    BEng in Civil Engineering

    University of Bristol

    Engineering seemed like a more logical and approachable choice at the time.

  • 2004-2005

    MSc in Environmental Management

    University of Bristol

    My dissertation titled "Whole Life Costing for Water Framework Directive" was an applied research project that led to my employment with SEAMS. The project had attracted significant interests from water industry and eventually led to three related MSc dissertations and one funded EngD project.

  • 2005-2010

    Asset Management Consultant

    SEAMS

    I was responsible for analysing huge amount of asset data (often in different formats and of varying quality), identifying trends, building integrated mathematical models and running large scale optimisation. My main duties were to provide clients better understanding of their assets and to provide evidence-based options for strategic investment planning. During my time in Sheffield, I also taught "Whole Life Costing" (a Master level technical module) at University of Sheffield and co-supervised three MSc students for their dissertation project.

  • 2010-2015

    EngD in Hydroinformatics

    STREAM Industrial Doctorate Centre

    In 2010, I returned to academia to pursue an Engineering Doctorate with STREAM. I have developed a novel decision support system for sustainable drainage design optimisation. The outcomes of my project are a doctorate thesis for University of Exeter and new software applications for XP Solutions. The project and its applications have already attracted interests from UK, US, Australia and China. A conference presentation and the follow-up workshops led to successful software sales in China.

  • Sep 2012

    Best Young Researcher Award

    Urban Drainage Modelling

  • Mar 2014

    Introducing 'CrimeMap'

    LondonR Talk

    Following the Data Analysis online course, I decided to improve my data visualisation skills in my spare time. This led to the creation of a blog, an web application 'CrimeMap' and my first R package 'rCrimemap'. These tools attracted some interests from the data science community. I was then invited to give a LondonR talk and to present a poster at the annual R user conference in 2014.
